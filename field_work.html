Field Work Experience

Below are highlights from my geological field work experiences at terrestrial analog sites for planetary research:

  • Euphrata Fan Boulder Field, Channeled Scablands, Washington

    Columbia River Basalt Province | October 2024

    Collecting measurements of boulder heights and diameters.

    Geological field work at Euphrata Fan boulder field, Washington. Measuring boulders to ground truth GIS measurments and #D products made in the lab. Analysis of these clasts will aid in computing flood magnitudes and serve as an analog to ancient outburst floods on Mars. Field activities included:

    • Mapping and collecting coordinates of largest boulders
    • Measurement of boulder dimensions
    • Analysis of boulder distributions and composition across the landscape

  • Additional Field Experience

    I have participated in several other field campaigns focused on planetary analog research, including:

    • Sedimentary structure analysis in paleolake environments
    • Geomorphological mapping of glacial features
    • Remote sensing ground-truthing for Mars analog terrain

    These field experiences have provided valuable context for interpreting remotely sensed data from Mars and other planetary bodies.

Field Work Experience

Below are highlights from my geological field work experiences at terrestrial analog sites for planetary research:

  • Moses Coulee Lava Field, Washington

    Columbia River Basalt Province | September 2023

    Geological field work at Moses Coulee lava field, Washington. Examining columnar basalt formations as analogs for similar structures observed on Mars. These formations provide insight into volcanic and cooling processes that may have occurred on Mars during its more geologically active periods.

    Conducted geological mapping and sample collection at the Moses Coulee lava field, part of the Columbia River Basalt Province. This site serves as an excellent analog for Martian volcanic terrain, particularly for understanding volcanic flow emplacement mechanisms and weathering processes. Field activities included:

    • Mapping of columnar basalt formations
    • Documentation of weathering patterns in basaltic terrain
    • Analysis of boulder distribution across the landscape
    • Collection of rock samples for laboratory analysis

  • Additional Field Experience

    I have participated in several other field campaigns focused on planetary analog research, including:

    • Sedimentary structure analysis in paleolake environments
    • Geomorphological mapping of glacial features
    • Remote sensing ground-truthing for Mars analog terrain

    These field experiences have provided valuable context for interpreting remotely sensed data from Mars and other planetary bodies.
